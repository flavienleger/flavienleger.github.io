Sinkhorn and the Schrödinger bridge problem

Schrödinger bridges

Consider the variational problem \begin{equation} \inf_{\rho,b} \int_0^1\!\!\!\!\int\frac{1}{2}|b_t(x)|^2\,\rho_t(x)\,dx\,dt, \end{equation} where the infimum runs over densities $\rho$ and vector field $b$ such that \begin{equation*} \partial_t\rho + \mathrm{div}(\rho b) = \gamma\Delta\rho, \end{equation*} and the boundary conditions $\rho_0=\mu$ and $\rho_1=\nu$ for fixed densities $\mu$ and $\nu$. The parameter $\gamma > 0$ is a diffusion coefficient. The resulting solution $\rho$ is called the Schrödinger bridge between $\mu$ and $\nu$.
