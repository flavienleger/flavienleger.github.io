Wasserstein gradient flows

The back-and-forth method [2] can be generalized to compute Wasserstein gradient flows [1], via the so-called JKO scheme [3]. This allows to efficiently solve a large class of interesting PDEs.

Let $\Omega=[0,1]^2$, we consider below various functionals $U(\rho)$ (both convex and convex+concave).

Porous medium equation

Let \begin{equation*} U(\rho) = \frac{\gamma}{m-1}\int_\Omega\rho(x)^m\,dx + \int_\Omega V(x)\,\rho(x)\,dx, \end{equation*} where $V\colon\mathbb{R}^2\to\mathbb{R}\cup\{+\infty\}$ is a potential energy. The Wasserstein gradient flow of $U$ is the PDE $$ \partial_t\rho +\mathrm{div}(\rho (-\nabla V))= \gamma\Delta \rho^m, $$ together with Neumann boundary conditions and an initial condition $\rho(0,\cdot) = \rho_0$.

Here $V(x)=\iota_{S}(x)+\frac{1}{2}\lVert x-a \rVert^2$, where $S$ is the star domain and $a$ the lower right corner.
